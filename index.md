This project investigates how new digital technologies can support the musical practice of Brazilian choro—a genre that emerged among Rio's working classes in the late 19th century. Our research combines musicological expertise with computational methods to develop AI-driven tools for harmonic analysis and automatic harmonization of choro melodies.

We are based at the [Julius-Maximilians-Universität Würzburg](https://www.uni-wuerzburg.de) and collaborate closely with the [Instituto Casa do Choro](https://acervo.casadochoro.com.br/) in Rio de Janeiro, home to the world's largest choro archive.

Read more on our [About](about) page.
